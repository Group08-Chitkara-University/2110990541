Retro's Chef Special Recipe

Bread Cutlet Quick Snacks

Indian style bread cutlet is delicious and liked by all kids. These bread cutlets can be served as party snack, packed for picnic or can be relished as an evening snacks. Heat 1 tsp of oil in a kadai/wok, add mustard seed and curry leaves. When mustard seeds start to splatter add mashed potatoes along with green chili, ginger, boiled peas, red chili powder, garam masla powder, dry mango powder and salt. Mix all the ingredients well and let it cook for 2-3 minute.

Retro's Guest Chef Special Recipe

Classic Avocado Croissant Club

In a non-stick pan, cook bacon until crispy, drain fat and set aside to cool. In a food processor, combine cooled bacon, flesh from 2 avocados, garlic and lime juice. Pulse until smooth then season to taste as needed with salt and pepper. To build sandwiches, spread avocado aioli on one half of each croissant and mustard seed on the other half. Layer meat, lettuce, tomato, avocado slices and cheese on bottom half of croissants then add top half of croissants. Serve as desired, whole or cut in half with toothpicks to keep together.

Retro's Customer Special Recipe

Caprese Croissant Breakfast Sandwich

Recipe makes 4 Servings. 1 Tbsp fresh garlic, minced, 3 Tbsp lime juice, 4 large butter croissants, cut in half, Place a slice of ham over the bottom piece of each croissant. Top each with a heaping 1/2 cup of scrambled eggs and 1/4 cup of cheese. Place the top half of each croissant over the sandwich. To serve immediately: Arrange a rack in the middle of the oven and heat to 350°F.
